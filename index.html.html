Robo-Dawn Elevator

Overview

Problem statement

The robot team needs a Microsoft surface to move up and down giving them the ability for Dawn to come face-to-face with someone or “sit” at a desk. It also has the benefit of temporarily lowering her center of gravity while traveling across the office.

Proposed work

Build an elevator with a carriage that raises and lowers using a motor that is fixed at the base (keeps CoM low) and ideally does not require a cascading (complicated) lift system. I believe that a traversing carriage attached to a fixed pole can have a CoM almost as low as a fully retracted cascading lift system.

Success criteria

  • Moves quickly up and down (2 seconds bottom to top)
  • Maintains a low CoM (doesn’t tip while retracted)

User stories

Raises MS to eye level when talking to a standing person

Lowers to eye level when talking to a sitting person

Lowers to screen level when looking at a desktop computer

Hides by lowering into a protected box when traveling long distance

Scope

Requirements

Current project requirements.

  • The overall system must be lightweight
  • The motor must be able to lift a Microsoft surface, against friction
  • The overall system should not raise the CoM to a point of tipping concern at walking speeds/accelerations

Alternatives considered: Cascading vs. Fixed Pole

Cascading Mechanisms

Unfortunately, all of these mechanisms have a mechanical complexity that (1) makes it difficult to design, manufacture, assemble, and maintain and (2) have mechanical complexity that requires stiffer and more complicated parts higher up, which pushes their CoM higher than theoretical possible with a cascading system and increase the overall weight.
